Raspberry Pi;often used as a cost-effective and powerful platform for developing embedded systems prototypes. Its GPIO (General Purpose Input/Output) pins allow it to interface with other electronic components, making it popular among hobbyists and professionals for various embedded applications.
Field-programmable gate array (FPGA);an integrated circuit designed to be configured by a customer or a designer after manufacturing â€“ hence the term "field-programmable." FPGAs contain an array of programmable logic blocks and programmable interconnects, allowing designers to create custom digital circuits for specific tasks. Context in Embedded Systems: FPGAs are widely used in embedded systems for tasks requiring high-speed data processing, parallel processing, and real-time processing. They are especially useful for applications where custom hardware acceleration is needed, such as signal processing, cryptography, and high-performance computing in embedded systems.
Arduino;an open-source electronics platform based on easy-to-use hardware and software. Arduino boards are equipped with microcontrollers and a development environment that simplifies the process of creating interactive electronic projects.
Supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA)/industrial control system (ICS);refers to a control system architecture that uses computers, networked data communications, and graphical user interfaces for high-level process supervisory management. SCADA systems are used to monitor and control industrial processes in sectors such as manufacturing, energy, water, and transportation.
Facilities;ICS systems in facilities control and monitor various building functions, such as HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning), lighting, and security systems.
Industrial;ICS is used to monitor and control manufacturing processes, ensuring efficiency and quality in production.
Manufacturing;ICS in manufacturing environments is crucial for managing production lines, robotics, and other automated processes.
Energy;ICS systems are used in the energy sector to monitor and control power generation, transmission, and distribution facilities, including power plants and electrical grids.
Logistics;ICS in logistics involves controlling and optimizing the movement of goods, vehicles, and materials within supply chains, warehouses, and distribution centers.
Internet of Things (IoT);refers to the network of physical devices (things) embedded with sensors, software, and other technologies that enable them to connect and exchange data with other devices and systems over the internet. IoT devices can range from everyday household appliances to industrial machinery.
Sensors;IoT devices are equipped with various sensors (such as temperature sensors, motion sensors, and environmental sensors) that collect data from the physical world. These sensors enable IoT devices to perceive and respond to their environment.
Smart devices;everyday objects enhanced with IoT capabilities. Examples include smart thermostats, smart locks, and smart refrigerators. These devices can be controlled remotely and often have automation features.
Wearables;technology devices that can be worn as accessories or clothing. Examples include smartwatches and fitness trackers. Wearables collect data about the user (such as heart rate and activity level) and often sync this data with other devices or applications.
Facility automation;devices and systems within buildings or facilities (such as lighting, security cameras, and HVAC systems) are interconnected and can be controlled and monitored remotely via the internet. This automation improves energy efficiency and security.
Weak defaults;IoT devices, especially in their early implementations, often come with default settings and passwords that are weak and easy to guess. Weak defaults refer to default configurations that lack robust security measures, making these devices vulnerable to attacks. It is important to change default passwords and configure IoT devices securely to prevent unauthorized access.
Medical systems;advanced healthcare technologies and devices designed for specific medical purposes. These systems include equipment such as MRI machines, CT scanners, robotic surgery systems, and patient monitoring devices. Specialized medical systems often involve complex software and hardware integration for precise diagnostics and treatment.
Vehicles;advanced technologies integrated into automobiles, trucks, buses, and other types of vehicles. These systems include features such as advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS), autonomous driving capabilities, in-car entertainment systems, and telematics. Specialized vehicle systems enhance safety, navigation, and overall driving experience.
Aircraft;encompass sophisticated technologies used in aviation. These systems include avionics, flight control systems, navigation equipment, and communication systems installed in airplanes and helicopters. Specialized aircraft systems are critical for flight safety, navigation, and communication with air traffic control.
Smart meters;specialized devices used for measuring and monitoring utility usage, such as electricity, gas, and water. Unlike traditional meters, smart meters are equipped with communication capabilities that allow remote monitoring and data collection. Smart meters enable utilities and consumers to track usage patterns, promote energy efficiency, and facilitate accurate billing.
Voice over IP (VoIP);a technology that allows voice communication and multimedia sessions over the internet, enabling phone calls, video conferences, and other forms of communication over IP networks. VoIP converts voice signals into digital data packets for transmission over internet protocol networks.
Heating, ventilation, air conditioning (HVAC);control indoor environmental conditions, including temperature, humidity, and air quality, to ensure comfort and safety. These systems are widely used in residential, commercial, and industrial buildings.
Drones;unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), are aircraft operated remotely by human pilots or autonomously by onboard computers. Drones have various applications, including aerial photography, surveillance, agriculture, and package delivery.
Multifunction printer (MFP);a device that combines several office functionalities into one machine, including printing, scanning, copying, and faxing. MFPs are commonly used in office environments.
Real-time operating system (RTOS);an operating system designed to manage hardware resources and execute tasks with strict timing requirements. RTOS is commonly used in embedded systems and applications where real-time processing is critical, such as industrial automation and automotive systems.
Surveillance systems;use cameras, sensors, and other technologies to monitor and record activities in specific areas. These systems are used for security, safety, and monitoring purposes in various environments.
System on chip (SoC);an integrated circuit that incorporates all components of a computer or electronic system into a single chip. SoCs are commonly used in mobile devices, IoT devices, and embedded systems.
5G;the fifth generation of mobile networks and telecommunications technology. It is designed to provide faster data transfer speeds, lower latency, and increased network capacity compared to previous generations (such as 4G/LTE). 5G technology enables a wide range of applications, including IoT devices, augmented reality, and autonomous vehicles.
Narrow-band;a type of wireless communication where the bandwidth of the transmitted signals is narrow. Narrow-band communication is often used in IoT devices and applications where a small amount of data needs to be transmitted over long distances with minimal power consumption.
Baseband radio;the transmission of digital signals directly without modulation onto a carrier frequency. Baseband communication is used in wired and wireless communication systems to transmit digital data in its original form without modulation.
Subscriber identity module (SIM) cards;a small card inserted into mobile phones and other cellular devices that securely stores the international mobile subscriber identity (IMSI) and the keys used to encrypt and authenticate calls on the mobile network.
Zigbee;a wireless communication standard designed for low-power, short-range communication between devices. It is commonly used in IoT applications for home automation, smart lighting, and industrial control systems.
Power;limitations related to the availability, consumption, and distribution of electrical power within a system. Devices, especially portable and IoT devices, must operate within specific power constraints to ensure proper functionality and longevity of the battery life.
Compute;limitations in processing power and computational capabilities of devices. Some devices may have limited processing abilities, affecting their ability to perform complex computations or tasks.
Network;limitations in bandwidth, connectivity, and data transfer rates within a network. Networks can experience congestion, latency, and limited throughput, impacting the speed and efficiency of data transmission.
Crypto;limitations in the implementation and processing of cryptographic algorithms. Some devices may lack dedicated hardware support for encryption, leading to slower encryption and decryption processes.
Inability to patch;situations where devices or systems cannot be easily updated or patched with security fixes. This constraint often arises in legacy systems or embedded devices that lack mechanisms for receiving and applying software updates.
Authentication;limitations in verifying the identity of users or devices. Weak or insufficient authentication mechanisms can lead to unauthorized access and security breaches.
Range;limitations in the distance over which devices can communicate wirelessly. Wireless technologies like Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and RFID have specific ranges within which they can establish reliable connections.
Cost;limitations in budget and financial resources allocated for security measures. Organizations may have limited funds to invest in security technologies, training, and personnel.
Implied trust;situations where devices or entities are automatically trusted without proper verification or authentication. This often occurs within closed or trusted networks, where devices assume trust without validation.
